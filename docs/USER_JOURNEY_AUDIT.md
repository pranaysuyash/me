# User Journey Audit: pranaysuyash.com

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Persona Evaluation:** 
1. **Hiring Manager** (Path: Hire Me)
2. **Founder wanting AI prototype** (Path: Work With Me)

---

## 1. Path: Hire Me (Hiring Manager)

### Is the path clear within 10s?
**Yes.** The link is prominent in the navigation, and the landing page for this path is focused on role-related value.

### What is the next action you would take?
- **Primary:** Book a 15-minute call.
- **Secondary:** Download the resume to verify technical details and history.

### What is missing before you would act?
- **Immediate Skill Stack:** For a hiring manager/recruiter scanning fast, a clear bulleted list of "Core Proficiencies" (e.g., Python, TypeScript, React, Docker, OpenAI/Anthropic APIs) is missing from the top fold of the Hire Me page.
- **Direct Proof of Work:** While "Work" is in the nav, a "Top Projects" highlight section *on this page* would reduce the friction of having to click away to see if the "Role fit" claims are backed by shipped artifacts.

---

## 2. Path: Work With Me (Founder)

### Is the path clear within 10s?
**Yes.** The "Build a pilot together" and "Build a scoped pilot in weeks" hooks are very effective for a founder's mindset.

### What is the next action you would take?
- Click "Send a short brief."

### What is missing before you would act?
- **Pricing Ballpark:** Founders are budget-conscious; a "Pilots typically range from $Xk to $Yk" or "Minimum engagement starting at $X" would filter leads and provide a baseline for budget discussions.
- **Client Logos/Social Proof:** Direct testimonials or logos of companies/founders already worked with would build major trust.
- **Success Metrics:** What happened *after* the pilot? (e.g., "The pilot led to a $1.2M seed round" or "The MVP was used by 500+ users").

---

## Friction & Drop-off Points

### 1. Dual-Nav Redundancy
The top navigation has "Work," "Hire Me," and "Work With Me."
- **Friction:** Having both "Hire Me" and "Work With Me" creates a cognitive split. The user has to choose between two similar-sounding CTAs. 
- **Recommendation (Blunt):** Merge these into a single "Work With Me" or "Hire" path and then provide the "Full-time vs. Pilot" choice on that page.

### 2. Drop-off Moment: The Pilot Form
Founders hate long forms. 
- **Drop-off:** If the "short brief" link goes to a generic contact form or Calendly without a clear prompt on what information is needed (e.g., "Tell me your problem, no technical jargon needed"), they might bounce.

### 3. Confusion After Clicking
- **What happens next?** There is no mention of response time (e.g., "Response in <24h"). 
- **Confusion:** Does "Book a call" go straight to a meeting or a request for a meeting? If it's a direct booking, it needs to say "Pick a time on my calendar."

### 4. Technical Fog
- **Drop-off:** For a hiring manager specifically, if they don't see the specific keywords they were told to find (e.g., "Next.js," "Cloud Functions") on the first scroll of the "Hire Me" page, they will drop off and move to the next candidate. The current page relies too much on the resume download for these details.

---

## Final Perception
The site is high-trust but high-friction in navigation. The "Work With Me" path feels slightly more cohesive as a service offering, while the "Hire Me" path feels like a standard job application page that requires too much browsing to find proof.
