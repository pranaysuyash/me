## STAGE 2: QUALIFICATION

### BANT Framework

**Budget:** Can they pay $3K-10K?
- Ask: "What's your budget range for this project?"
- Red flag: "We don't have a budget yet" (tire kicker)
- Green flag: "$5-10K range" (qualified)

**Authority:** Can they sign the check?
- Ask: "Who else needs to approve this project?"
- Red flag: "I need to check with my team" (not decision maker)
- Green flag: "I'm the founder/CTO, I decide" (qualified)

**Need:** Do they have a real problem?
- Ask: "What happens if this problem isn't solved?"
- Red flag: "Would be nice to have" (low priority)
- Green flag: "We're losing $X/day" (high priority)

**Timeline:** Do they need it soon?
- Ask: "When do you need this delivered?"
- Red flag: "Sometime this year" (not urgent)
- Green flag: "Need it before board meeting next month" (urgent)

### Qualification Scorecard

| Criteria | 1 (Poor) | 2 (Fair) | 3 (Good) | Score |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Budget | < $3K | $3-5K | $5K+ | ___ |
| Authority | None | Influencer | Decision maker | ___ |
| Need | Nice to have | Important | Critical | ___ |
| Timeline | 3+ months | 1-3 months | < 1 month | ___ |
| **Total** | | | | **/12** |

**Score:**
- 10-12: Hot lead (prioritize)
- 7-9: Warm lead (nurture)
- < 7: Cold lead (deprioritize)

## STAGE 3: DISCOVERY CALL (15-30 min)

### The Goal
Understand the problem deeply enough to scope and price

### The Structure

**1. Rapport (2 min)**
```
"Thanks for taking the time. How did you hear about me?"
"Tell me a bit about [Company]—what do you do?"
```

**2. Problem Exploration (10 min)**
```
"What problem are you trying to solve?"
"Why is this important now?"
"What have you tried already?"
"What's the business impact of solving this?"
"What happens if you don't solve it?"
```

**3. Technical Constraints (5 min)**
```
"What's your current tech stack?"
"Any integration requirements?"
"Compliance/security constraints?"
"Internal resources who'll maintain this?"
```

**4. Success Criteria (3 min)**
```
"What does 'done' look like?"
"How will you measure success?"
"What would make this project a win for you?"
```

**5. Timeline & Budget (5 min)**
```
"When do you need this delivered?"
"What's driving that timeline?"
"What's your budget range for this?"
```

**6. Next Steps (2 min)**
```
"Based on what you've shared, this sounds like a [project type]. 
I'll put together a proposal with scope, timeline, and investment. 
Can I send that by [day]?"
"Any questions for me before we wrap?"
```

### Discovery Call Scorecard

**After the call, rate:**
- Clarity of problem: ___/5
- Urgency: ___/5
- Budget fit: ___/5
- Personal fit: ___/5

**Total > 15:** Send proposal  
**Total 10-15:** Send light proposal or more info  
**Total < 10:** Politely decline

## STAGE 5: CLOSING

### Handling Objections

**"The price is too high"**
```
"I understand budget is a concern. A few options:
1. Reduce scope to fit budget—what's most critical?
2. Phased approach—build MVP first, then iterate
3. Equity component—if you're open to it

What would work best for you?"
```

**"We need to think about it"**
```
"Of course. What questions can I answer to help with the decision? 
Is it the scope, timeline, or something else?"
```

**"We want to compare with other vendors"**
```
"Makes sense. A few things to compare:
• Time to delivery (I deliver in weeks, not months)
• Technical expertise (YC alum, 14 years, recent AI work)
• Availability (I can start immediately)

Happy to chat further after you've reviewed options."
```

**"Can you start without the upfront payment?"**
```
"I require 50% upfront for new clients. This covers initial work and 
ensures commitment on both sides. I've found projects without upfront 
payment tend to lose momentum.

However, I'm happy to break it into milestones if that helps:
• 25% to start
• 25% at midpoint
• 50% on completion

Would that work?"
```

---

### The Close

**When they're ready:**
```
"Great! I'll send over the agreement now. Once you sign and pay the 
upfront (50%), I'll begin work within 48 hours. Excited to build this 
with you!"
```

**Send immediately:**
- Contract/agreement

## STAGE 6: DELIVERY & COLLECTION

### Payment Collection

**Upfront (50%):**
- Send invoice immediately after contract signed
- Begin work only after payment received
- No exceptions (cash flow is critical)

**Final (50%):**
- Deliver work for review
- Get sign-off on completion
- Send final invoice
- **Hold code/assets until final payment** (leverage)
- Deliver everything after payment received

**If Payment Delayed:**
```
"Hi [Name], just following up on the final invoice. Once payment is 
received, I'll transfer the final assets and documentation. Let me 
know if there are any questions!"
```

**If Payment Seriously Delayed (>2 weeks):**
```
"Hi [Name], I haven't received payment for the final invoice. Per our 
agreement, I need to pause any additional work until this is resolved. 
Happy to discuss a payment plan if that helps."
```

## RED FLAGS (Walk Away)

**Don't take the project if:**
- ❌ Can't pay 50% upfront
- ❌ Scope is vague and keeps changing
- ❌ Unrealistic timeline ("Need it yesterday")
- ❌ No decision maker in the room
- ❌ Previous vendors failed (pattern)
- ❌ Gut feeling says "trouble"

**Your time is valuable. Protect it.**

---

**Bottom Line:** Sales is a numbers game. 10 leads → 3 proposals → 1-2 deals. Keep the pipeline full.
