## 1. Audit Findings

### **Generic Phrases & Filler**
- **"Working systems":** Used as a default state. A system is only a system if it works. (Tautology)
- **"Something usable fast":** "Something" is the weakest word in a copywriter's vocabulary.
- **"Actually":** Overused 8+ times (e.g., "actually use," "actually realistic"). It's a verbal tic that signals a lack of confidence in the primary statement.
- **"Real":** Used to qualify "problem" and "workflow." If it's not a real problem, it's not a problem.

### **Vague AI Language**
- **"Applied AI prototypes":** "Applied AI" has become a semantic black hole. Specify the function (e.g., extraction, orchestration, indexing).
- **"Workflow-heavy":** Descriptive but lacks the edge of "operational drag" or "process toxicity."

### **Repetition Fatigue**
- **"Workflow":** Mentioned 22 times across three pages.
- **"Messy/Unstructured":** The "Messy → System" hook is used identically in both the Homepage hero and the Work With Me subheader. Repetition across pages is okay; repetition within the eye-span of a single user journey is lazy.

## 2. The "Worst 5" Rewrites (Sharper Versions)

| Original | Brutal Rewrite | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1.** `I turn messy workflows and unstructured inputs into working systems.` | **"I convert fragmented workflows and unstructured data into production-ready software."** | "Messy" is too informal for $10k+ builds. "Production-ready" is the senior-level promise. |
| **2.** `...teams need something usable fast — including reducing insurance processing from 4 weeks to 10 days.` | **"...high-stakes internal tools for document-heavy operations—like reducing insurance processing by ~60%."** | "Something usable fast" is fluff. Leading with "High-stakes" and specific reduction percentages has more teeth. |
| **3.** `A few projects that show how I scope, build, and ship applied AI and workflow-heavy systems.` | **"Engineering proof spanning document extraction, semantic search, and operational automation."** | "A few projects that show how I..." is throat-clearing. Get straight to the technical artifacts. |
| **4.** `The best fit is a real workflow problem, a narrow first version, and a team that wants something useful quickly.` | **"I specialize in high-impact bottlenecks: converting manual document handling into automated pipelines with a 2-week V1."** | "Something useful quickly" is the definition of a filler phrase. "Bottlenecks" and "Pipelines" are the language of your buyer. |
| **5.** `AI prototypes, workflow automation, and internal tools scoped for measurable first outcomes.` | **"Functional AI pilots and automation systems scoped for immediate ROI."** | "Measurable first outcomes" is consultant-speak. "Immediate ROI" is clear, aggressive, and commercial. |

- **Filler Word Count:** Removed ~14 instances of "actually," "just," and "real."
- **Repetition:** Reduced the "Workflow" count by ~40% in key headers to prevent word-blindness.
