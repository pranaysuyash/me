# Co-Founder Conflict Exit Strategy: Sensitive Navigation Guide

**Situation:** Co-founder at MedPiper, internal rift, non-communication with other co-founders, being sidelined, company failing  
**Goal:** Exit professionally, preserve reputation, land softly  
**Date:** April 1, 2026  
**Classification:** Confidential / Personal Strategy

---

## The Real Situation (Internal Frame)

**What's Actually Happening:**
- You're a co-founder at a YC-backed startup
- There's a rift between co-founders
- Non-communication with other co-founders
- You're being excluded/sidelined
- Company is likely shutting down
- You need to leave for your own wellbeing

**What You Need to Avoid:**
- Looking like you abandoned ship
- Looking like you caused the problem
- Looking bitter or unprofessional
- Burning bridges with YC network
- Legal/complication risks

**What You Need to Signal:**
- Professional operator who can handle tough situations
- Experienced builder who delivered despite challenges
- Person who knows when to move on
- Asset to any team, not liability

### What You NEVER Say (Even if True)

❌ "My co-founders stopped talking to me"  
❌ "I was pushed out"  
❌ "The company is a mess"  
❌ "I don't know what's happening"  
❌ "There's a lot of drama"  
❌ "I had to leave because of conflict"  

**Why:** This makes you look like:
- Someone who couldn't resolve conflict
- Difficult to work with
- Potential source of future drama
- Unaware of business basics

---

### What You DO Say (All True, But Strategic)

✅ "I'm exploring new opportunities after 4 years building MedPiper"  
✅ "Led MedPiper from 0 to $1M ARR through multiple phases"  
✅ "Now looking for senior IC or staff roles where I can contribute"  
✅ "Open to consulting while finding the right full-time fit"  
✅ "Healthcare SaaS and applied AI experience"  

**Why:** This positions you as:
- Experienced operator
- Selective (not desperate)
- Forward-looking
- Professional

## Red Flags to Avoid in Your Story

### 🚩 Desperation Signals

**Don't Show:**
- Urgency about finding a job
- Willingness to take any role
- Complaining about current situation
- Badmouthing co-founders or company

**Instead Show:**
- Selectivity about roles
- Multiple options (job + consulting)
- Professionalism about past
- Forward-looking optimism

---

### 🚩 Drama Signals

**Don't Show:**
- Hints of conflict or problems
- Vagueness that suggests hiding something
- Defensiveness when asked about MedPiper
- Reluctance to discuss the situation

**Instead Show:**
- Pride in accomplishments
- Willingness to discuss details
- Professional handling of transition
- References who can vouch for you

---

### 🚩 Incompetence Signals

**Don't Show:**
- Confusion about company status
- Blame-shifting to co-founders
- Lack of clarity about your role
- Revisionist history about accomplishments

**Instead Show:**
- Clear metrics and achievements
- Ownership of what you built
- Understanding of business realities
- Honesty about the transition
